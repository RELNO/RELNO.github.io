In traditional Jewish synagogues, a physical barrier called a “septum” historically separated spaces for men and women, reflecting theological beliefs held over centuries. This competition explored a different approach, one that promotes greater equality while respecting religious principles. The project introduced a novel perspective on the septum: instead of acting only as a divider, it became a functional space that fosters unity. The reimagined septum supports circulation, lighting, and book storage within the synagogue. As a result, it takes on a more communal function during synagogue rituals while highlighting its significance as a symbol of unity.
